Why Small Businesses Are Switching to VoIP

March 27, 2026 · 4 min read

In today’s fast-paced, digital-first business environment, effective communication isn't just a luxury—it’s the backbone of your success. Small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs) need to leverage every technological advantage they can to stay competitive against massive corporations.

Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) technology offers a powerful, enterprise-grade solution for modern communication needs, without the enterprise price tag.

At Nemerald, we understand the unique challenges faced by growing teams. In this post, we’ll explore the top three benefits of upgrading your small business to a VoIP cloud phone system, including massive cost savings, hybrid-work mobility, and frictionless scalability.

1. Drastic Cost Savings​

One of the most significant advantages of VoIP for small businesses is the immediate impact on your bottom line. Traditional on-premise PBX (Private Branch Exchange) phone systems are notoriously expensive to install, configure, and maintain. They require massive upfront investments in copper wiring, server racks, and ongoing costs for line rentals.

VoIP, on the other hand, routes your calls securely over your existing internet connection, effectively bypassing traditional telecom monopolies.

  • Lower Monthly Phone Bills: VoIP services typically offer vastly lower rates for local and long-distance calling. Providers like Nemerald offer unlimited calling plans within the US, Canada, and Mexico that can drastically reduce your monthly telecommunications budget.
  • Zero Hardware Maintenance: With a cloud phone system, there’s no need for a physical PBX server in your IT closet. All you need is a reliable internet connection and VoIP-compatible IP desk phones—or you can skip the hardware entirely and just use your existing smartphones and laptops.
  • No Setup Fees or Telecom Technicians: Setting up a VoIP system is plug-and-play. You don't need to pay a technician $150/hour to run cables through your office walls.

2. Improved Communication & Mobility​

VoIP is no longer just about making voice calls. Modern Unified Communications (UCaaS) platforms offer a suite of features that drastically enhance how your team collaborates.

  • Enterprise-Level Features Included: VoIP systems come standard with advanced routing features that used to cost thousands of dollars. You instantly get access to multi-level auto-attendants (IVR), voicemail-to-email, call queues, custom hold music, and AI-powered call transcriptions.
  • Built for Remote and Hybrid Work: VoIP untethers your business from the physical office. Employees can make and receive business calls from anywhere in the world using an internet connection. By downloading the Nemerald mobile or desktop app, your team's smartphones and laptops become their office extension.
  • Enhanced Collaboration: With integrated features like team messaging, SMS, and video conferencing, VoIP fosters better collaboration. You can transfer live calls to a remote coworker just as easily as if they were sitting at the desk next to you.

3. Frictionless Scalability​

As your business grows, your communication needs will evolve. Traditional phone systems punish growth—adding a new employee often means waiting weeks for the phone company to run a new physical line to your building.

VoIP systems are inherently scalable, designed to grow exactly at the pace of your business.

  • Add or Remove Lines in Seconds: With a cloud-based admin portal, provisioning a new phone number or adding a new employee extension takes just a few clicks. There is no need for complex installations or new server hardware.
  • Customizable Plans: VoIP providers offer flexible service tiers. Whether you’re a local startup with three employees or a scaling agency with a 50-person contact center, you only pay for the exact number of seats (users) you need that month.
  • Future-Proof Technology: Because the "brains" of a VoIP system live in the cloud, software updates and new features (like AI insights and CRM integrations) are pushed to your system automatically. Your business will always stay ahead of the curve without having to buy new hardware.

Ready to make the switch?​

Upgrading to VoIP technology offers undeniable benefits for small businesses. From massive cost reductions to empowering your remote workforce, cloud communications are the standard for modern business.
